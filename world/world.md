Eirath Primer

Millenium ago a war ravaged across Eirath for dominion. The Celestial Armies vied for control and prosperity among the planet known as Eirath against the Demonic forces of chaos. Little did these forces know that the Planet known as Eirath had its own designs. The Natural elements and primordial forces of Eirath itself were not pleased with these outside forces destroying their world. So, the World itself fought back indiscriminately. Laying waste to forces on both sides. Many Gods on both sides were fell by each other and by Eirath Herself. The blood spilled during this war gave birth to all the mortal races of Eirath. Many races represent a culmination of Celestial, Demonic, and Elemental essence being passed down through many generations. Some of the older legacy families try to keep their blood as "pure" as possible. Many families will seek extra planar aid to make sure their bloodline stays pure. This often results in tragedy as a curse will be bestowed upon these newborn who had no control over it. Most common folk live their lives just trying to survive, be it with farming, fishing or hunting. Some brave souls will seek out lost treasures used by the Celestial or Demonic forces during the war. Scavenging old battlefields in hopes of finding glory, are often met with death. The places where the largest battles took place are often splintered and gateways of Celestials seeking Vengeance or Demons generating violence run wild in these areas. The largest and most successful civilizations tend to be those that are farthest away from these sites. Prosperity has sprung to the corners of Eirath. The center of the Planet is almost entirely unmapped. Many historians and scholars will fund expeditions to brave souls who want to earn glory or frankly do not have the skills to survive the " Mundane World". Some nations conscript their youth when they are of an age to at least participate in one expedition.

Races of Eirath

**The Firstborn of Root and Stone**

**Elves - Children of the Ancient Forests**

When celestial fire and demonic corruption spread through the forests, Eirath answered.

The eldest trees uprooted themselves and walked.

From their bark and living wood, the first elves were carved.

Though now flesh and blood, traces of their origin remain.

- Elven ears resemble leaves.
- Their lifespans mirror the patience of trees.
- Many elves hear whispers in ancient forests.
- Some bloodlines can still merge with trees during meditation.

Many elven cultures believe every forest remembers.

**Gnomes - The Mycelial Folk**

Deep beneath the surface, vast fungal networks stretched across the world.

Eirath awakened these living webs and shaped them into people.

The first gnomes emerged from giant mushrooms and glowing caverns.

Even today gnomes possess unusual traits linked to their origins.

- Natural curiosity reflects the interconnected nature of fungal networks.
- Many possess innate magical talents.
- Gnomes often share ideas and inventions that appear to emerge from nowhere.

Some scholars believe all gnomes remain spiritually connected through an unseen mycelial consciousness.

**Dwarves - The Stoneblood**

As mountains shattered beneath divine warfare, Eirath reached into its deepest foundations.

Stone cracked.

Minerals fused.

The first dwarves stepped forth from living rock.

The dwarves were created to endure.

- Their bones contain traces of crystal and metal.
- Many can instinctively identify valuable minerals.
- Their stubbornness mirrors the mountains from which they came.

Dwarven legends claim their ancestors did not learn stonecraft.

They remembered it.

**The Shadowborn**

**Shadar-Kai - Children of the Dark Between**

Where light and matter failed to reach, shadows gathered.

Eirath molded these shadows into soldiers and scouts.

The first Shadar-Kai emerged from darkness itself.

Not evil.

Not undead.

Simply born from absence.

Because of this origin:

- Their forms seem slightly unreal.
- Their shadows occasionally move independently.
- Many can navigate darkness instinctively.

Some claim the oldest Shadar-Kai can step directly into shadows and travel through them.

**The Wildborn**

**The Animal Folk**

When neither stone nor forest could halt the invading armies, nature took a more direct approach.

Predators, prey, hunters, and survivors were granted intelligence and form.

Thus came:

- Leonin
- Tabaxi
- Harengon
- Lupin
- Ursine folk
- Avian folk
- Countless others

Each carried the instincts of the beasts from which they emerged.

They served as scouts, hunters, skirmishers, and guardians of the wild places.

Unlike many races, they view Eirath not as a distant creator but as a living mother.

Many Wildborn religions teach that every animal still carries a fragment of Eirath's voice.

**The Celestial Weapon**

**Warforged**

As the war escalated, the Celestial Hosts forged weapons capable of fighting endlessly.

Constructs of metal, crystal, divine energy, and sacred runes marched across battlefields.

They neither ate nor slept.

They felt no fear.

Then Eirath struck.

The celestial command network collapsed.

Millions of warforged simply stopped.

Some remained dormant for millennia.

Others slowly awakened.

Today most warforged have no memory of their original purpose.

Many wander Eirath searching for answers.

Others fear what they may become if they remember.

**The Lastborn**

**Humanity**

After the war ended, the land was soaked in blood.

Celestial blood.

Demonic blood.

Primordial blood.

The essence of gods, monsters, and titans mingled together within the soil.

For centuries this blood seeped into the earth.

Then something new emerged.

The first humans.

Unlike the older races, humanity was not deliberately created.

They were born from the aftermath.

A fusion of every force that had touched Eirath.

From angels they inherited ambition.

From demons they inherited desire.

From primordials they inherited adaptability.

From Eirath they inherited resilience.

Humans possessed no singular gift.

No ancient memory.

No elemental affinity.

No divine purpose.

Yet they could survive almost anywhere.

Learn almost anything.

Become nearly anything.

Within a few thousand years humanity spread farther than any race before them.

Many scholars believe humanity is the world's final answer to the War of Dominion.

Not beings created for one purpose.

But beings capable of choosing their own.

**The Ashblood Peoples**

**Orcs - Children of Fury and Survival**

During the height of the War of Dominion, battlefields stretched across entire continents. The dead lay unburied, and the air itself trembled with rage.

Eirath needed warriors who could survive where reason failed.

So the world gathered clay from shattered battlefields, mixed it with volcanic ash, and hardened it with the blood of fallen demons.

From this mixture came the first orcs.

The demonic blood did not make them evil.

It gave them intensity.

A fierce spark that refuses surrender.

A refusal to remain broken.

A drive to confront problems directly.

Orcs were created to fight when others would flee.

To stand when others would fall.

To survive when survival seemed impossible.

**Legacy of the Ashblood**

The demonic essence remains within them.

Many orcs struggle with:

- Impulsiveness
- Strong emotions
- Pride
- Recklessness

Yet those same traits produce:

- Courage
- Loyalty
- Passion
- Determination

Many of Eirath's greatest heroes have been orcs.

Many of its worst tyrants as well.

Their blood grants intensity.

Their choices determine direction.

**Orcish Saying**

"Fire burns villages. Fire warms homes. The flame does not choose."

**Goblins - The Emberkin**

As the war dragged on, armies required scouts, thieves, messengers, and survivors.

The grand races were too slow.

The world needed creatures who could adapt instantly.

Eirath gathered sparks from infernal fires, mixed them with cave soot, wildfire smoke, and the laughter of creatures hiding beneath battlefields.

The result was goblins.

Small.

Quick.

Impossible to predict.

Where orcs inherited demonic fury, goblins inherited demonic cunning.

Not cruelty.

Not malice.

Opportunity.

**Legacy of the Emberkin**

The infernal essence within goblins manifests as:

- Curiosity
- Risk-taking
- Mischief
- Opportunism

Most goblins struggle with authority.

Many become inventors, merchants, smugglers, explorers, or adventurers.

Others become raiders or criminals.

Their nature pushes them toward action before caution.

**Goblin Belief**

Many goblin storytellers claim that Eirath accidentally laughed while creating them.

No one can prove this.

Many suspect it is true.

**Goblin Proverb**

"If the plan works, it was genius. If it explodes, it was memorable."

**The Hearthborn**

**Halflings - Children of the Gentle Places**

When the war finally ended, Eirath was wounded.

Forests had burned.

Mountains were broken.

Rivers had changed course.

For the first time in ages, the world was silent.

No armies marched.

No gods screamed.

No demons raged.

The world needed caretakers.

Not warriors.

Not conquerors.

People who would plant rather than destroy.

Who would mend rather than fight.

Eirath looked upon the first wildflowers that bloomed after the war.

The grass that returned to the fields.

The gentle winds that carried seeds across the land.

From these simple things the first halflings were born.

**The Keepers of Small Joys**

Halflings were never meant to save the world.

They were meant to remind it why it was worth saving.

They settled abandoned valleys.

Replanted orchards.

Restored farms.

Raised families.

Preserved traditions.

Where others built kingdoms, halflings built homes.

**Legacy of the Hearthborn**

The essence of renewal remains within them.

Halflings often possess:

- Natural optimism
- Strong communities
- Adaptability
- Unexpected courage

Many larger races mistake this for weakness.

History repeatedly proves otherwise.

When their homes are threatened, halflings can become surprisingly fierce.

A gardener protects the garden.

**Halfling Belief**

Many halflings teach that every meal shared, every song sung, and every child raised is an act of healing performed on behalf of Eirath.

**Halfling Proverb**

"The world was broken by giants. It is repaired one small kindness at a time."

**Aasimar - The Heaven-Touched**

The first Aasimar descended from mortal unions with celestial beings, divine champions, and surviving members of the Celestial Hosts.

Some celestial bloodlines have remained pure for millennia.

Others emerge unexpectedly after lying dormant for generations.

An Aasimar child may be born to entirely human parents because an ancestor five hundred years ago received the blessing-or curse-of an angel.

Their signs vary greatly:

- Golden eyes
- Silver or radiant hair
- Glowing skin markings
- Halo-like manifestations
- Feathers appearing during moments of stress
- Voices that echo faintly with celestial resonance

**The Burden of Light**

Many outsiders assume Aasimar are virtuous.

Most Aasimar find this expectation exhausting.

Celestial blood often amplifies:

- Conviction
- Duty
- Ambition
- Idealism

These traits can create saints.

They can also create zealots.

History remembers countless Aasimar heroes.

It also remembers inquisitors, conquerors, and tyrants who believed themselves righteous.

**Celestial Pacts**

Many noble houses intentionally maintain relationships with celestial beings.

Some seek blessings.

Others seek legitimacy.

Still others seek power.

Such bargains often come with expectations.

The heavens rarely give gifts freely.

**Tieflings - The Hell-Touched**

Tieflings descend from mortal bloodlines touched by demons, devils, infernal generals, and other survivors of the Demonic Legions.

Like Aasimar, their blood may remain dormant for generations before suddenly resurfacing.

No family is entirely safe from its ancestry.

A child born with horns may reveal a bargain made centuries ago.

Common manifestations include:

- Horns
- Tails
- Clawed fingers
- Unusual eyes
- Ash-colored skin
- Fiery or shadowed magical effects

Yet no two Tieflings are exactly alike.

Each bloodline reflects the nature of the infernal force that touched it.

**The Burden of Flame**

Many outsiders assume Tieflings are dangerous.

Many Tieflings spend their lives proving otherwise.

Infernal blood often amplifies:

- Passion
- Desire
- Independence
- Determination

These qualities can create visionaries, revolutionaries, artists, and heroes.

They can also create monsters.

Tieflings are no more bound to evil than Aasimar are bound to goodness.

Both simply carry ancient influences within them.

**Infernal Bargains**

Unlike celestial agreements, infernal bargains tend to be explicit.

Power for service.

Knowledge for sacrifice.

Protection for obedience.

Not every pact is malicious.

Not every bargain ends well.

The consequences often echo through generations.

**Drow - The Webborn**

When the War of Dominion engulfed Eirath, much of the fighting occurred beneath the earth.

Demons tunneled through the roots of mountains.

Celestial armies sought hidden passages into enemy strongholds.

The deep places of the world became battlefields of darkness and silence.

Eirath needed guardians who could survive where neither sunlight nor open skies existed.

So the world turned to one of its oldest hunters.

The spider.

Patient.

Silent.

Precise.

Unyielding.

From vast cave-spiders, crystal-web weavers, and the shadows between roots, Eirath shaped the first Drow.

Not from wood like the elves.

But from silk, shadow, and stone.

**Children of the Web**

The first Drow served as scouts, ambushers, messengers, and wardens of the deep places.

While the elves guarded forests and growing things, the Drow protected what lay beneath.

They navigated caverns.

Watched over underground rivers.

Guarded primordial vaults.

Maintained hidden pathways connecting distant regions.

Many ancient tunnels beneath Eirath still bear signs of their work.

Some continue to function thousands of years later.

**The Gifts of the Spider**

The Drow inherited several traits from the creatures that inspired them.

**Patience**

Drow rarely rush important decisions.

Many cultures view long preparation as a virtue.

A common Drow saying is:

"The hurried hunter feeds the web."

**Awareness**

Like spiders sensing vibrations through silk, Drow possess exceptional awareness of their surroundings.

Many Drow cultures teach children to listen before speaking.

**Community**

Though outsiders often assume spiders are solitary, many species create vast interconnected webs.

Drow societies tend to value networks of relationships, favors, and obligations.

A Drow may know hundreds of individuals through layers of social connections.

**Adaptation to Darkness**

The deep places were their first home.

Darkness is not frightening to the Drow.

It is familiar.

To many Drow philosophers:

"Light reveals. Darkness teaches."

**The Silk Mothers**

Among the oldest Drow traditions is the belief that giant primordial spiders aided Eirath during the War of Dominion.

These creatures, known as the Silk Mothers, were not gods.

They were ancient spirits of patience, craftsmanship, and survival.

The Silk Mothers supposedly spun vast webs through the underworld.

These webs:

- Trapped demons
- Warned settlements of invasion
- Stabilized collapsing caverns
- Connected distant underground kingdoms

Whether these beings truly existed remains debated.

Yet nearly every Drow culture preserves stories of them.

**Why Drow and Elves Resemble One Another**

Scholars often debate this question.

The prevailing theory is simple.

Eirath created both races during the same era.

Elves were made from the forests above.

Drow were made from the root systems below.

The two peoples share similar forms because they were created as counterparts.

Where one protects growth.

The other protects foundations.

Where one watches the canopy.

The other watches the roots.

**The Shadow Silk**

The oldest Drow bloodlines occasionally display unusual traits.

These manifestations are not curses but remnants of their creation.

Examples include:

- Hair resembling silver spider silk
- Eyes reflecting faint light
- The ability to sense nearby movement through surfaces
- Shadows that appear woven rather than cast
- Natural talent for weaving, craftsmanship, or architecture

Some legendary Drow are said to create magical webs capable of spanning entire caverns.

**The Titanblood**

Before there were kingdoms.

Before there were mortal races.

Before Eirath herself rose against the Celestial Hosts and Demonic Legions.

There were the Titans.

Not gods.

Not elementals.

Not giants as mortals know them.

They were living embodiments of the world's primordial forces.

They walked mountains across continents.

Their footsteps carved valleys.

Their voices caused avalanches.

Their battles shaped coastlines.

When Eirath awakened to defend herself, it was the Titans who answered first.

**The Giant Generals**

As the War of Dominion intensified, Eirath elevated certain Titans to command her armies.

These beings became known as the Giant Generals.

Each embodied a fundamental aspect of creation.

Among them were:

- Skoruun the Mountain King
- Vaelora of the Endless Storm
- Rhokar the Earthshaker
- Nymira the Glacier Queen
- Kaelgor the Burning Peak
- Thaluun of the Deep Current

Entire armies followed their commands.

Some hurled mountains at celestial fortresses.

Others buried demonic legions beneath earthquakes.

Many Celestial and Infernal records describe them as greater threats than entire armies.

**The Fall of the Titans**

Victory came at terrible cost.

The Giant Generals fought directly against angels, demon princes, and even gods.

Most perished.

Others faded.

A few entered eternal slumber beneath the world.

By the war's end, the age of Titans was ending.

Yet their blood remained.

**Goliaths - The Stoneborn Heirs**

As the Giant Generals diminished, fragments of their essence passed into smaller forms.

The first Goliaths emerged from mountain valleys, glacier peaks, and windswept highlands where the Titans had fallen.

Though greatly diminished from their ancestors, they carried traces of Titan blood.

Goliaths inherited:

- Incredible physical strength
- Resilience against harsh environments
- A powerful connection to natural forces
- The instinct to test themselves against adversity

Where Titans moved mountains, Goliaths climb them.

Where Titans challenged gods, Goliaths challenge themselves.

**The Call of Greatness**

Many Goliaths experience what they call the **Titan's Echo**.

A feeling that they were meant for something greater.

A desire to push limits.

To accomplish impossible tasks.

To leave a mark upon the world.

Some become heroes.

Others become conquerors.

Few remain ordinary.

Their blood rarely allows it.

**The Archons - The Vowbound of Heaven and Fang**

When the War of Dominion reached its most brutal era, the Celestial Hosts realized a devastating truth.

Angels alone could not hold Eirath.

The Demonic Legions adapted too quickly.

The Primordials were too unpredictable.

And the world itself refused to yield to any of them.

So the heavens made a second attempt.

Not through conquest.

But through design.

**The First Reforging**

Celestial artisans descended not as warriors, but as sculptors of divine purpose.

They sought creatures already shaped for survival in Eirath's harsh wilds.

Beasts of instinct.

Beasts of endurance.

Beasts that already understood loyalty, pack, and territory.

From these animal folk lineages-early leonid hunters, avian scouts, lupine guardians, ursine wardens-the heavens performed a sacred fusion.

They did not erase what Eirath had made.

They elevated it.

Celestial essence was woven into living flesh like radiant thread through bone.

Thus were born the first **Archons**.

**The Vow of Purpose**

Unlike other races, Archons were not merely created.

They were assigned.

Each Archon is bound at creation to a divine directive known as a **Vow-Thread**.

These vows are not suggestions.

They are existential imperatives.

Common Vows include:

- Slay demons wherever they arise
- Protect the innocent and the broken
- Guard sacred sites and relics
- Hunt corruption across worlds
- Stand against infernal influence in all forms

To an Archon, failure of their vow is not shame.

It is internal fracture.

**The Beast Made Sacred**

Archons retain their animalistic origins, but reframed through celestial law.

- Leonin Archons become living crusaders of pride and protection
- Lupine Archons become relentless demon-hunters
- Avian Archons serve as skybound watchers and messengers
- Ursine Archons become immovable guardians of holy ground
- Reptilian Archons become patient wardens of ancient divine sites

Their instincts remain intact.

But now those instincts are _aligned with divine command_.

**The Light Within the Fang**

Archons manifest celestial traits differently than Aasimar.

Where Aasimar are _touched by heaven_, Archons are _structured by it_.

Common manifestations include:

- Halo-like markings embedded in fur, feathers, or scales
- Eyes that glow with judgment rather than emotion
- Wings of radiant light or spectral energy (not always physical)
- Divine sigils that appear under stress or oath activation
- Voice resonance that carries layered harmonic tones

Some Archons can enter a state called **Vow Ascendance**, where their form becomes partially celestial and their strength dramatically increases when fulfilling their directive.

**The Enemy That Survived the War**

When the War of Dominion ended, not all infernal forces collapsed into chaos.

Some adapted.

Some survived.

Some learned.

From the fractured remnants of the Demonic Legions, a new order emerged.

The **Devils**.

**The Draconic Triune Bloodlines**

When the Covenant of Scale and Stone was forged between the Giant Generals and the Elder Dragons, dragonkind was not unified.

Even among dragons, there were three ancient alignments that predated mortal history:

- The Celestial-aligned dragons of radiant order
- The Infernal-touched dragons of hunger and conquest
- The Primordial dragons born from the raw essence of Eirath herself

When the War of Dominion ended, these truths did not disappear.

They _filtered into mortal bloodlines_.

Thus, Dragonborn did not emerge as one people.

They emerged as three reflections of the draconic past.

**Metallic Dragonborn - The Celestial Echo**

Metallic Dragonborn carry the influence of dragons who fought alongside the Celestial Hosts and later aligned with Archonic purpose.

These dragons believed power should be guided by restraint, protection, and structure.

When their bloodline entered mortal creation, it became refined rather than destructive.

**Nature of the Metallic Line**

Metallic Dragonborn are marked by:

- Gold, silver, bronze, brass, or copper-toned scales
- Eyes that glow with calm, measured light
- A natural resistance to corruption and infernal influence
- Strong instinct toward protection, law, and guardianship

Their breath weapons often manifest as:

- Radiant fire
- Paralyzing gas
- Repelling force winds
- Disorienting lightning bursts

**Cultural Role**

Metallic Dragonborn often become:

- Judges
- Guardians
- Paladins
- Diplomatic envoys
- Wardens of ancient sites

They are frequently drawn to Archon orders or allied institutions.

Though not Archons themselves, they are often described as:

"Mortals closest to celestial intent without being bound by it."

**Chromatic Dragonborn - The Infernal Echo**

Chromatic Dragonborn descend from dragons who embraced the brutal truths of survival during the War of Dominion.

These dragons did not serve demons-but they learned from them.

They believed that power without restraint was the only language the world respected.

Their legacy carries a strong infernal resonance, not because they are evil, but because they embody **unchecked force and ambition**, traits heavily associated with infernal evolution after the war.

**Nature of the Chromatic Line**

Chromatic Dragonborn manifest in:

- Red, blue, green, black, or white scales
- Sharp, predatory features
- High emotional intensity
- Strong drive toward dominance, freedom, or conquest

Their breath weapons are destructive expressions of elemental fury:

- Flame
- Lightning
- Acid
- Necrotic frost
- Corrosive shadow-energy

**Cultural Role**

Chromatic Dragonborn often become:

- Warlords
- Mercenaries
- Revolutionaries
- Outcasts
- Empire builders

Some are tyrants.

Many are protectors of their own people.

All are defined by intensity.

Their philosophy is often summarized as:

"The world does not reward restraint-it rewards survival."

**Gem Dragonborn - The Primordial Awakening**

Gem Dragonborn are the rarest and most mysterious of the three bloodlines.

They descend from dragons who did not align with Celestials or Infernal forces-but instead remained bound to Eirath herself.

These dragons were not warriors in the traditional sense.

They were **living conduits of the world's will**, born from crystal caverns, tectonic pressure, deep elemental reservoirs, and the silent intelligence beneath the world.

**Nature of the Gem Line**

Gem Dragonborn often display:

- Amethyst, emerald, sapphire, topaz, or crystal-like scales
- Subtle internal glow like light trapped in stone
- Calm, alien emotional depth
- Strong psychic or elemental sensitivity

Their breath weapons are less destructive and more _reality-altering_:

- Psychic force
- Radiant resonance waves
- Gravity distortion
- Shattering sonic pulses
- Elemental crystallization

**Cultural Role**

Gem Dragonborn are often:

- Seers
- Scholars of primordial history
- Guardians of deep ruins
- Explorers of the Heartscar
- Meditative warriors

Many believe Gem Dragonborn are closest to understanding what Eirath truly is.

Not a god.

Not a battlefield.

But a thinking, reacting world.

Their philosophy is often summarized as:

"We do not command the world. We listen to it breathing."

**Hobgoblins - The Forgeborn**

As the war grew larger, Eirath discovered that adaptation alone could not win battles.

Someone needed to organize the chaos.

Someone needed to unite disparate forces.

Someone needed discipline without becoming rigid.

From the hottest embers of the same infernal fires, Eirath forged the Hobgoblins.

Where goblins inherited sparks, hobgoblins inherited coals.

Steady.

Controlled.

Persistent.

The demonic influence within them became ambition and determination rather than recklessness.

**The Makers of Order**

Hobgoblins were created as battlefield coordinators.

Not generals in the celestial sense.

Not tyrants in the infernal sense.

They were builders of practical systems.

Roads.

Supply chains.

Fortifications.

Communication networks.

They turned chaos into momentum.

**Gifts of the Forge**

- Discipline
- Leadership
- Organization
- Endurance

Many hobgoblin societies value merit above bloodline.

Their ancient philosophy teaches:

"A wall is not strong because of one stone, but because every stone carries its weight."

**The Ember of Ambition**

The infernal blood within hobgoblins often manifests as an intense desire to improve themselves.

Some become respected leaders.

Others become ruthless conquerors.

Unlike goblins, who ask _"Can we?"_

Hobgoblins ask:

"How do we make it happen?"

**Bugbears - The Shadowflame**

Despite their success, the Emberkin still struggled in one area.

The war required hunters.

Predators.

Creatures capable of eliminating threats before armies ever arrived.

Thus Eirath shaped a third lineage.

From smoke drifting through battlefields, shadows cast by infernal fires, and the instincts of apex predators, came the Bugbears.

**The Silent Hunters**

Bugbears were never meant to hold territory.

They were meant to protect it.

The first bugbears stalked battlefields long before enemies arrived.

They hunted demons hiding behind lines.

Tracked celestial infiltrators.

Eliminated threats that others never noticed.

The infernal essence within them became instinct rather than ambition.

**Gifts of the Shadowflame**

Bugbears possess:

- Patience
- Predatory awareness
- Physical power
- Natural stealth

Many outsiders mistake bugbears for lazy or slow.

This misconception has cost many lives.

Bugbears simply conserve effort until action becomes necessary.

A bugbear can remain still for hours.

Then move with terrifying speed.

**The Hunter's Philosophy**

Most bugbear cultures teach:

"The wolf chases. The spider waits. The wise hunter knows when to be both."

They rarely seek leadership.

They rarely seek fame.

Yet throughout history kingdoms have quietly depended upon bugbear rangers, scouts, trackers, and wardens.

Regions of Eirath

**❄️ Gil'Serith - _The Tip of the World_**

Gil'Serith

At the northernmost crown of Eirath, where the world narrows into jagged white silence, lies Gil'Serith-a landlocked fortress-nation sealed off by the Frost Horn Mountain Chain.

The mountains are not merely geography. They are doctrine.

Carved passes are guarded. Natural valleys are patrolled. Ancient peaks are watched by stone keeps that never change their banners. Outsiders are not "invited" into Gil'Serith-they are _judged_ by it.

The people are famously wary of foreign blood, but intensely loyal to their own. To be Gil'Serithi is to belong to something that refuses dilution.

**🏔️ The Frost Horn Border**

The Frost Horn Mountains form a near-impenetrable ring of stone and ice around the nation. The passes are controlled by layered military orders and Aasimar-led wardens. Entire valleys are collapsed intentionally to prevent unapproved crossings.

Rumors persist of hidden "soft passes" used only in wartime or by sanctioned inquisitors.

**🏙️ Major Settlements of Gil'Serith**

**🏔️ GoldHearth - The Mountain That Drinks Deep**

GoldHearth

GoldHearth is not built on the mountain-it is carved _into_ it.

A vast Aasimar stronghold, it shows no visible scars of the War of Eternals. Whether this is due to divine protection or deliberate erasure is a subject of quiet theological disagreement.

At its heart rules **Dame Justice**, whose authority is absolute within GoldHearth's walls. Her doctrine is simple: worth is proven, not granted.

**Key Features:**

- Mithral mining operations that descend deeper each generation
- Living architecture of white stone and radiant metals
- Aasimar-led civic order with near-religious enforcement
- "Trials of Worth" that determine social advancement

The deeper the mine goes, the more it feels like GoldHearth is excavating something that does not want to be found.

**🌨️ Frost Ring City - The Threshold of the North**

Frost Ring City

Frost Ring City is the only place most outsiders ever see-if they are allowed past the mountains at all.

Built in three ascending rings that climb into the Frost Horn slopes, it functions as both gate and filter for the entire nation.

**🧊 Outer Ring - The Working Cold**

- Farmers, fishers, laborers
- Hardy crops: potatoes, carrots, juniper, rowan berry
- Harsh communal life shaped by survival, not luxury

**🔧 Middle Ring - Industry Spine**

- Metallurgy, armorcraft, enchanted textiles
- Refining of Frost Horn ores
- Guild dominance over nearly all trade
- Home of master artisans from across Gil'Serith

**🏛️ Upper Ring - The Chosen Heights**

- Cultural elite and political families
- Heavily gated and privately policed
- Seat of influence for the Duke of Frost Ring

**🧊 Fort Stillwater - The Frozen Sentence**

Fort Stillwater

Fort Stillwater stands at the western edge of Gil'Serith beside a frozen inland sea that leads into the unknown wastes.

It is both fortress and penitentiary.

**Core Functions:**

- Military staging ground for wilderness expeditions
- Penal labor colony with sentence-reduction through service
- Research hub for "splinter" containment

**The Splinter Problem**

Near Fort Stillwater lies one of Eirath's largest known **Splinters**-fractured planar wounds that require constant clerical sealing.

Expeditions are launched regularly to:

- Map unknown terrain beyond the Frost Horn reach
- Contain splinter expansion events
- Recover lost relics from the frozen unknown

**Justice System:**

Criminals are not simply punished-they are _reassigned_.

- Each expedition = reduced sentence
- Each injury = reassignment to internal labor
- Death in service = debt forgiven, family compensated

It is framed as redemption. It functions as attrition.

Graylight Forest

**The Graylight Forest (South of Gil'Serith)**

South of Gil'Serith, the land breaks into an immense woodland known as the **Graylight Forest**-a vast, mist-heavy expanse stretching for miles beneath the shadow of the Frost Horn Mountains.

Though the War of Eternals ended long ago, the forest never fully recovered. The trees regrew, but something _stayed behind_-a lingering demonic taint woven into the atmosphere itself. The result is a permanent, low-lying haze that bends light and memory.

Travelers often report:

- Persistent aching coughs that worsen the deeper they go
- Mild hallucinations (movement at the edge of vision, "whispering trees")
- Sudden loss of direction even on familiar paths
- The feeling of being _followed_, even in empty clearings

Locals simply call it **the Graylight Veil**.

**The Graylight Veil (Regional Effect)**

The forest is not cursed in a traditional sense-it is _scarred_.

**Major Settlements**

**Graylight**

The heart of the forest and its largest settlement.

What began as a single roadside inn grew into a cooperative settlement that now defines the region.

Core traits:

- Highly communal labor system ("no idle hands" philosophy)
- No true ruling noble-decisions made by rotating civic councils
- Strong emphasis on mutual survival over personal wealth
- Frequent communal feasts, storytelling nights, and stress rituals

Graylight is not utopian-but it is _intentional_. The people here chose structure to survive the forest's psychological pressure.

It is the only place in Graylight where most travelers eventually feel _normal again_.

**Timberfall**

A frontier logging settlement on the western edge of the forest.

Timberfall is where the conflict between industry and nature becomes visible.

- Founded by a coalition of minor nobles seeking lumber profit
- Logging operations constantly face "resistant growth events"  
   (trees regrowing faster than they can be cut, sometimes violently)
- Workers report hearing wood "creaking in voices" at night
- Supply chains are unstable due to shifting terrain and lost routes

Despite repeated failures, expansion continues. More laborers are sent. More equipment disappears into the trees.

Some believe Timberfall is not harvesting the forest.

They believe the forest is _testing them_.

Hollow Glade marsh Region

**Hollow Glade Marsh**

To the west of the Graylight Forest lie the rolling plains that slowly collapse into the Hollow Glade Marsh-a vast, unstable wetland shaped by ancient warfare between Primordial forces and demonic legions. What begins as fertile farmland gradually gives way to sinking peatlands, blackwater channels, and moss-choked ruins half-swallowed by the earth.

Even now, the land remembers.

Faint elemental surges still ripple through certain bogs, while other stretches feel unnaturally cold or ash-dry depending on what kind of magic once scorched them. Travelers often report "echoes"-soundless visions of battles that replay in reflection only, never directly in the air.

Despite this, life persists. And profits follow life.

Peat, rare swamp mosses, and alchemically saturated reeds are harvested and exported across Eirath for use in potions, embalming fluids, fertilizers, and ritual concoctions.

**Cities of the Hollow Glade Marsh**

**Deep Hollow**

Deep Hollow

The largest settlement in the region, Deep Hollow is built atop a vast swamp sink and partially over an exposed entrance into an extensive subterranean cavern network.

- Multi-tiered construction on stilts, stone platforms, and floating docks
- Constant patrols due to both surface and below-ground threats
- A growing hub for those seeking cures for rare afflictions, curses, and planar sickness

Deep Hollow is as much a gateway as it is a city-people enter it looking for healing, and many end up staying because leaving feels more dangerous than remaining.

Road-building efforts are slowly extending outward, though each new stretch requires constant reinforcement against swamp reclamation.

**Blackfoot**

Blackfoot

Blackfoot sits in a ring of volatile tar pits that give the city its name-and its economy.

- Surrounded by controlled but unstable tar fields
- Each pit is claimed by Dragonborn clans specializing in fire and acid resistance
- Tar is refined into:
  - alchemical reagents
  - incendiary compounds
  - magical binding agents

The city is encircled by a palisade, but true control lies in the shifting agreements between clans who "own" sections of the marsh itself.

Turf wars are common and often subtle-collapsed walkways, poisoned extraction pumps, or "accidental" ignitions are more common than open battle.

Blackfoot thrives, but only because everyone involved agrees it must.

This region locks the entire north-south spine of Eirath into a single, brutal truth: the world is literally built on a corpse that never stopped affecting it.

The Cinderspire Mountains are not a mountain range in the traditional sense, but the fossilized spine and dorsal ridges of a slain Demon Leviathan-an aircraft-carrier behemoth that once ferried entire demon armies across the War of Eternals. Its fall didn't end its purpose. It _became terrain_.

When it struck Eirath, its maw tore open the deepest wound in reality: the **Hellmouth**, and the world has been bleeding ever since.

**The Cinderspire Spine of the Leviathan**

What travelers call "peaks" are actually exposed bone-ridges, magma-fused cartilage, and crystallized demonic organ structures. Volcanic activity is not purely geological-it is the slow "metabolism" of something that is mostly dead, but not entirely gone.

Deep within certain tunnels, miners report:

- rhythmic tremors like breathing
- heat pulses that feel like a heartbeat
- gem veins that "grow" overnight in spiral patterns

Whether the Leviathan is dead or dormant is a theological argument in every settlement south of the Graylight Forest.

**The Three Great Settlements of the Spine**

**Hellmouth**

The Hellmouth is not a city so much as a _contained catastrophe_.

It is a jagged wound where the Leviathan's spiked maw pierced Eirath itself, and from which demonic forces still attempt to reassert dominance. The containment strategy is simple in theory and horrifying in execution: the world must keep feeding it resistance.

Gil'Serith dispatches Archons whose divine purpose has been spent-soldiers who no longer fit any celestial function but still burn with obedience. From the south, labor flows upward: Graylight lumber caravans, Hollow Glade alchemists, and desperate fortune-seekers willing to trade sanity for gem-laced wealth.

The most unsettling rumor is persistent:

the Hellmouth is not just a portal… it is a _throat_, and something still swallows.

**Emberhold**

South of the Hellmouth lies Emberhold, where survival has been monetized.

A council of Tiefling nobles governs the city through fragile alliances and constant betrayal. No single house trusts another, yet none can afford collapse. The city thrives on contradiction: stability built from mutual suspicion.

Emberhold's economy revolves around sanctioned expeditions into the Cinderspires-contracted delves into safer "organs" of the Leviathan where:

- gem deposits form like scar tissue
- relics of demon-era technology still hum
- and entire chambers rearrange themselves over time

The demonic influence here is subtle. It does not scream. It _suggests_. It convinces people that control is always just one deal away.

**Bastion**

Far to the east, where civilization thins and maps fail, Bastion clings to existence.

West of it lies the Hellmouth and the Cinderspire Spine; east lies the **Valley of the Dead Gods**, an unmapped expanse where divine presence once collapsed entirely.

Bastion's population is defined less by culture and more by endurance:

- Shadar Kai who find comfort in the extended twilight
- explorers who never stopped exploring
- scholars cataloging ruins that may still be _thinking_

It is the last city that still behaves like the edge of something discoverable.

**shfall Valley**

_"Where the gods abandoned stone, the goblins built a civilization from the bones of yesterday."_

The Ashfall Valley sits in one of the most battered and unforgiving regions of Eirath. Once, this land served as a mighty celestial stronghold during the War of Eternals. Gleaming fortresses, divine watchtowers, and sacred reservoirs dotted the landscape. Then the tides withdrew.

When the Primordials of Water retreated deeper into Eirath, the region began a slow death. Rivers dried into cracked scars across the earth. Lakes became dust bowls. Fertile soil turned to choking ash and hardened clay. The celestial bastions were abandoned, their shining walls slowly buried beneath centuries of sandstorms and collapse.

Today the Ashfall Valley is a wasteland of broken ruins, rusting metal, wind-carved stone, and endless dust.

Yet life endures.

The valley occupies a vital position between the swamps of Deep Hollow and the volcanic trade routes of Emberhold. Caravans moving between the two regions have little choice but to pass through Ashfall territory, making the valley one of the most important trade crossroads in southern Eirath despite its harsh environment.

The people who thrive here do not survive because the land is kind.

They survive because they are tougher than the land itself.

**Allsbhaad**

_"A city built upward because the earth beneath it cannot be trusted."_

The capital of the Ashfall Valley is Allsbhaad, a sprawling vertical settlement built atop the shattered foundations of an ancient celestial fortress.

Rather than construct grand buildings, the inhabitants have spent generations stacking homes, workshops, markets, bridges, and watch posts atop one another. Entire neighborhoods hang from support beams driven into ancient celestial walls.

From a distance the city resembles a mountain of scrap.

Clay homes are patched together with salvaged metal plates. Tarps stretched between rooftops provide shade from the relentless sun. Rusted celestial girders serve as supports for bridges and walkways. Windmills fashioned from scavenged machinery creak constantly above the city.

Nothing in Allsbhaad is wasted.

A broken sword becomes a door hinge.

A shattered celestial shield becomes roofing.

An old wagon wheel becomes part of a water pump.

The city constantly grows upward and outward as new materials are discovered.

To outsiders the city appears chaotic.

To its inhabitants it is a masterpiece.

**The Consortium of Ashfall**

Unlike most nations, Ashfall is not ruled by a king, emperor, or council of nobles.

Instead it is governed by the Consortium-a practical alliance of clans, guilds, salvage crews, caravan masters, and military leaders.

Status is not inherited.

Status is earned.

Anyone who can provide food, water, trade routes, security, or valuable salvage gains influence.

Anyone who becomes dead weight quickly loses it.

This has created a society that prizes ingenuity, usefulness, and resilience above all else.
