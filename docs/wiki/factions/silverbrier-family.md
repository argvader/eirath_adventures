# The Silverbrier Family

**One of the five founding families of [Timberfall](../locations/timberfall.md)**

## Overview

Timberfall was founded by five wealthy families, all competing for the same thing: the right to cut down the [Graylight Forest](../locations/graylight-forest.md), haul out the lumber, and sell it. The Silverbriers are one of them, and they have a particular reputation — cheap, careless, and the likeliest of the five to send untrained people further into the woods than anyone should go. As Icarus put it, having worked for them before: they always send you on the dangerous jobs, and they never pay well.

They are the reason twelve people are missing.

## Leadership

**Lady Silverbrier** holds the house in Timberfall. She is a *second* daughter — no inheritance is coming to her — and she was posted out here largely against her will to run the family's logging concern while the rest of them enjoy **Deep Hollow**. Greedy, and openly indifferent to the common folk. Asked why she'd sent twelve loggers into a forest everyone agrees is deadly without waiting two days for the escort she had already hired, the family's own head of security shrugged: paying idle workers for two weeks would be *insane*. She has the money. She simply didn't feel like spending it.

The party has not met her. That meeting has been floated, and has not yet happened.

## Known Members

- **[The head of security](../npcs/silverbrier-head-of-security.md)** — the smug human at the gate who does the family's hiring and none of its risking.

## Current Activities

- Logging the Graylight, badly and at other people's expense.
- Recovering the twelve missing loggers — cheaply. They have hired **two** parties for this now and intend to pay only whichever one produces living loggers first. The party (twenty gold a head) is competing directly against [Miriam Dawn](../npcs/miriam-dawn.md)'s group (thirty-five gold a head), who went out a week ago and have not come back.
- They will pay **nothing** for the rescue of the first rescue party.
- If both parties fail, the plan is simply to hire more desperate labor out of Deep Hollow and keep sending them in until someone figures out what is out there.

## Relationship with the Party

Employer, grudgingly, on both sides. Icarus has a history with them and it is not a warm one: he was hired as the escort for this expedition, arrived two weeks late, and was fired for it. Wilonet took the job purely for the money and has said so out loud. Grano thinks the whole enterprise is foolishness and has said *that* out loud, in Dwarvish, to a drow, in front of them.

Icarus is currently quartered in the family's barracks at the estate.
