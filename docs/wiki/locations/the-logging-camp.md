# The Logging Camp

Four or five days into the [Graylight Forest](graylight-forest.md), the trees open onto a
cluster of canvas tents, a scatter of felled timber, and axes left lying exactly where they
were dropped. This is the cut site the [Silverbriers](../factions/silverbrier-family.md) sent
twelve loggers to work, and it has been standing empty for two weeks. Nothing has been looted.
Nothing has been broken. There is no blood, no struggle, and no bodies — the crew simply
stopped being here, and the forest has been quietly moving in on the clearing ever since.
On a desk near the fire ring, a worn journal lies open where its writer left it.

## What's Here

- **The tents**, still pitched, enough for a crew of twelve.
- **The axes**, dropped rather than stacked.
- **[Halvik Dern](../npcs/halvik-dern.md)'s journal** — the foreman's log, and the only
  account of what happened to the twelve. It ends mid-thought: *I think we're still alive. I
  think we're still here.*
- **Rope.** Coils of it, fifty feet a length, which is genuinely the most useful thing the
  party took out of the place.

Beyond that, a typical working camp with the value stripped out of it. No potions, nothing
magical, nothing that would fetch a price.

## The Footprints

Three sets of tracks come into the camp on top of the loggers' own, and they are wrong for
loggers — heavier, armored, not workmen's boots. They are also **much more recent**: days old
against the crew's week and a half. This is [Miriam Dawn](../npcs/miriam-dawn.md)'s rescue
party, and they got here first.

The armored tracks lead into the camp and then **split**, staggering off in two separate
directions. One set is noticeably smaller than the other. Whatever happened here, that group
did not leave together.

The party chose the smaller set.

## The Watch

They camped in the dead camp, relit the loggers' own fire, and each of them saw something
during their watch. None of the three saw the same thing, and none of them was ever attacked.

- **[Wilonet](../pcs/wilonet.md), first watch.** A figure walked up out of the dark, took one
  of the axes, and began to chop — and made **no sound at all** when the blade went into the
  wood. It worked a while, set the axe down, stood there, and thinned away.
- **[Icarus](../pcs/icarus.md), second watch.** An orc woman came into the camp, shrugging
  something off her shoulders that was not there. He was reaching to wake the others when he
  saw she was **translucent**. She put her forehead to a tree and started beating her head
  against it — and the tree splintered, chunks coming off it under blows with nothing behind
  them. Every detail of it matches what Dern wrote about [Cara](../npcs/cara.md).
- **[Grano](../pcs/grano.md), third watch.** People — plural, unnumbered — scattering out of
  the camp in every direction, and not there when he looked straight at them.

In the morning, the axe was exactly where it had been left the night before. The tree was
splintered, and nobody could say how fresh the damage was.

## What It Means

The [Graylight Veil](graylight-forest.md) bends light and memory, and the standard reading is
that all three watchmen simply hallucinated the journal they had just read. The awkward detail
is the tree. The bark came off it while Icarus watched something that wasn't there hit it, and
the damage was still there at sunrise.

Dern's last written line was *I think we're still here.* The camp appears to agree with him.
