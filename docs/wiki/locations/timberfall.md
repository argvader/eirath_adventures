# Timberfall

A logging settlement of barely fifty or sixty souls, hammered together on the eastern edge of the [Graylight Forest](graylight-forest.md) by five wealthy families who have never once swung an axe themselves. They pay the poor, the desperate, and the merely aimless to walk into the trees and cut, and the trees have begun to take them. Mud streets, a single sagging tavern, a temple barely worth the name, and beyond the last fence the fog stands waiting like a wall.

## The Five Families

Timberfall was founded by five families, all of them competing for the same prize: the right to cut the Graylight down, haul the lumber out, and sell it. The rivalry is the town's real government. The [Silverbriers](../factions/silverbrier-family.md) are the family the party has dealings with, and by all accounts they are the worst of the five — the ones most willing to send untrained people too deep for too little coin.

## Who You'll Find Here

- [Jonas Dawn](../npcs/jonas-dawn.md) — the town's healer, of the small [Church of the Dawn](../factions/church-of-the-dawn.md) temple. Currently drinking himself sideways at the [Fat Goat](the-fat-goat.md) over his missing sister.
- [Old Marnie](../npcs/old-marnie.md) — an ancient elf alchemist who keeps a hut at the edge of town and knows more about the forest than anyone living.
- The [Silverbrier head of security](../npcs/silverbrier-head-of-security.md) — a smug, salt-and-pepper human who stands at the estate gate and does not, under any circumstances, go into the woods himself.

## The Missing

Twelve loggers — orcs, humans, a gnome or two — were sent two or three days deep into the Graylight and have not been heard from in two weeks. In a town this size that is a fifth of the population. People vanish in the Graylight now and then, one or two at a time, when they wander too far. Twelve at once has never happened before.

A first rescue party of four went out roughly a week ago and has also not returned.

## Elsewhere

Timberfall is a backwater, and everyone here knows it. The place people would rather be is **Deep Hollow**, a larger settlement some distance off — where the Silverbriers wish they were stationed, where fresh desperate labor can always be hired, and where a mercenary might be posted between jobs.

South or above, depending on who you ask, stand the **Cinderspire Peaks**, a hard range of mountains. There was a monastery up there once. There isn't anymore.
