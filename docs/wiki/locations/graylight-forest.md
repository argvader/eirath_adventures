# The Graylight Forest

An ancient forest of dark, dark brown wood and oppressive leaves, drowned at every hour in a pale standing fog that never burns off. The haze is not weather. It is residue — what settled here after the demons of the Abyss and the celestials of the heavens finished using this world as their battlefield, thousands of years ago, and it has never lifted since. The deeper in you go, the fewer feet have gone before you, and the angrier the forest gets.

## What It Does to You

Every account agrees on one thing: the Graylight plays tricks with your head. It shows you things that are not there and hides things that are. The fog makes it hard to see what is directly in front of you. [Old Marnie](../npcs/old-marnie.md)'s only real advice, delivered as the party left his hut, was this: **don't trust everything you see out there.**

Four days in, the party can be more specific than that. The haze thickens as you go, and as it thickens it starts **opening people up**.

- **It digs.** It does not show you the forest. It shows you *your own worst thing*, in full, while you are awake and walking. [Icarus](../pcs/icarus.md) lost most of an afternoon to a flashback of [a killing on the road](../npcs/the-old-farmer.md) and came out of it shaking, in front of the others, still marching.
- **It dreams at you.** [Grano](../pcs/grano.md)'s first night in produced **three separate dreams** of his monastery being destroyed — once by demons, once by a haloed figure who stopped a mace an inch from his skull and called him worthy, once by the forest itself coming up through the floor. He cannot tell which, if any, were memory.
- **It is not universal.** [Wilonet](../pcs/wilonet.md), who has ranged the shallow edges of this forest for centuries, has had **nothing**. No dreams, no visions, no flashbacks. Whatever the Veil is reaching for, it has not found a grip on her.
- **It leaves marks.** The unnerving part. At [the logging camp](the-logging-camp.md) all three of them saw silent, translucent figures working the clearing on their watches — and in the morning the tree one of them had been beating its head against was **genuinely splintered**, chunks off the bark, still there in daylight.

## The Forest Fights Back

The oldest rumor in [Timberfall](timberfall.md) — the one Old Marnie gives the most weight — is that the forest simply does not want to be cut down. The spirits in it have lain dormant a long time. Sometimes, he says, it bites back. And the further in you push, into the parts where almost nobody has ever walked, the more furious those spirits become.

Twelve loggers went two or three days deep, and vanished.

Their foreman wrote down how it started, and it was not teeth. The crew simply **lost the will to work**. A few refused outright and told him why: *the wood feels wrong*, and *the trees watch us back*. See [the logging camp](the-logging-camp.md) and [Halvik Dern](../npcs/halvik-dern.md)'s journal.

## Known Dangers

- **The wolves.** Ordinary wolves range the shallow reaches, and no one thinks much of them. What the party met on the first evening in was not ordinary. They **blink** — vanishing mid-lunge and reappearing behind you, or thirty feet away. Even beheaded, the body flickers out of existence before it drops. Nothing in Timberfall's memory has ever seen a wolf do this.
- **The Nightroot Asp.** A serpent, seven or eight feet of it standing, that lies along a trunk in **exactly** the shape and dark-blue color of a nightroot vine and holds still until you put a hand on it. It turns **invisible** at will, disengages without giving you a swing, and its bite carries a venom that will take you down if your constitution fails you. It nearly killed Grano outright: a natural 20, opened throat, thirteen damage, unconscious in one hit.
- **Whatever eats the dead.** The party came back to the blink dog corpses the next morning and found them chewed on. The bite marks were not canine and not sharp. Wilonet's read: **humanoid**.
- **The fog itself**, and whatever it lets you believe.

## Nightroot

A dark blue, vine-like plant that grows out in the Graylight. [Old Marnie](../npcs/old-marnie.md) forages for it and wants more — he'll turn it into "something" for anyone who brings it in, and sketched it out for the party before they left. What it becomes, he did not say.

**The party has it.** They also learned, the expensive way, that in the deep forest a nightroot vine and a **Nightroot Asp** are indistinguishable until one of them tenses under your hand. Wilonet did everything right — remembered Marnie's warning, stopped, rolled to check — and the forest simply lied to her well enough. When the fight was over, the actual root was lying a few feet away in plain view, and nobody wanted to touch it.

The snake, harvested, gave up **four vials of poison** and a set of scales that might make armor with something of its vanishing in it. The nightroot itself came out of the same clearing, tangled around the thing that was imitating it.
