# The Graylight Forest

An ancient forest of dark, dark brown wood and oppressive leaves, drowned at every hour in a pale standing fog that never burns off. The haze is not weather. It is residue — what settled here after the demons of the Abyss and the celestials of the heavens finished using this world as their battlefield, thousands of years ago, and it has never lifted since. The deeper in you go, the fewer feet have gone before you, and the angrier the forest gets.

## What It Does to You

Every account agrees on one thing: the Graylight plays tricks with your head. It shows you things that are not there and hides things that are. The fog makes it hard to see what is directly in front of you. [Old Marnie](../npcs/old-marnie.md)'s only real advice, delivered as the party left his hut, was this: **don't trust everything you see out there.**

## The Forest Fights Back

The oldest rumor in [Timberfall](timberfall.md) — the one Old Marnie gives the most weight — is that the forest simply does not want to be cut down. The spirits in it have lain dormant a long time. Sometimes, he says, it bites back. And the further in you push, into the parts where almost nobody has ever walked, the more furious those spirits become.

Twelve loggers went two or three days deep, and vanished.

## Known Dangers

- **The wolves.** Ordinary wolves range the shallow reaches, and no one thinks much of them. What the party met on the first evening in was not ordinary. They **blink** — vanishing mid-lunge and reappearing behind you, or thirty feet away. Even beheaded, the body flickers out of existence before it drops. Nothing in Timberfall's memory has ever seen a wolf do this.
- **The fog itself**, and whatever it lets you believe.

## Nightroot

A dark blue, vine-like plant that grows somewhere out in the Graylight. [Old Marnie](../npcs/old-marnie.md) forages for it and wants more — he'll turn it into "something" for anyone who brings it in, and sketched it out for the party before they left. What it becomes, he did not say.
