# Grano

**Player:** Darryl · **Ancestry:** Dwarf · **Class:** Cleric

*Last of his Monastery*

## Overview

Ninety-five years old, gray-haired, and very quiet. Chainmail, a mace, and a shield, carried with what can only be described as a soft-spoken elegance — he is not a loud man and does not become one under pressure. When the party first gathered at the job board it was Grano who actually asked the useful question: *how long have these twelve been missing?*

He mostly grumbles. The party has already worked out that when he does say something, it is worth hearing.

## What He Carries

Grano spent the last seven years alone. He is the last survivor of a monastery somewhere up in the **Cinderspire Peaks**, where everyone else was killed. He mentioned this once, flatly, to a stranger who had irritated him, and then said nothing further about it.

He has not explained what happened there. Nobody has asked.

## Faith

His holy magic is **bright, vibrant, cleansing, and plain** — his own words. His **sacred flame** falls on a thing and leaves it charred. In a forest full of gray fog and lying half-light, he is the only light in the party that is actually light.

He knew the [Church of the Dawn](../factions/church-of-the-dawn.md) on sight, and recognized its sigil and its theology of redemption and rebirth immediately — a very good roll on a subject he clearly already knew well. Which god he himself serves has not yet been said aloud.

## The Three Dreams

The first night in the [Graylight](../locations/graylight-forest.md), Grano fell into a heavy
sleep and dreamed three times, once per watch. He woke between each one, saw who was standing
guard, and went back under.

**First.** A church bell, twice. Then the monastery, whole — the forest's smells and sounds
draining away and being replaced by it. An old man walked up to him: *"Brother Grano, how's it
going this morning?"* Grano said he was tired, that he had been in a forest with companions and
had slain some beasts and closed his eyes and here he was. *"Interesting. Interesting."* And
then the old man's face **melted** — red skin, a demon's horns — and around him Grano could
hear his brothers being killed, shouting holy words into a fight they were losing. When he
opened his eyes in the dream there was nothing left but corpses in a ring around him.

**Second.** Pure white light, and a figure walking out of it with a **golden halo**.

> *We are faithful, are we not?*
> — *We are.*
> *Good. Good. Is your faith pure?*
> — *Yes.*

The figure raised an enormous mace and swung it down on him, and stopped it dead an inch short.

> *Maybe you will survive, brother Grano. My mace deems that you are worthy.*

**Third.** The monastery's tile floors bursting open with roots and vines. A tree coming up
through the middle of the building, cracking it, bringing the pillars down on his brothers as
nature took the temple back. Then no sound at all — just his brothers running, and falling, and
**turning into green and brown**, leaves and pine needles blowing apart where men had been
standing.

At the end of each of the three, he was the only one alive.

He does not know which of them were dreams, which were memories, or whether any of them were
either. He said so out loud, once, when the party finally compared notes: *"I don't know what
it is. Be it memory, dream, or future of my past. Something."*

The party has never heard how his monastery died. Grano has now watched it die three different
ways in one night.

## Temperament

Disinclined to suffer fools, and unbothered about hiding it. Told the [Silverbrier head of security](../npcs/silverbrier-head-of-security.md), to his face and without heat, *"I don't like you at all."* Thinks the whole logging enterprise is greed dressed up as commerce — steel is expensive, the war was foolishness, and these people rush off to danger without a thought for anyone involved.

## Relationships

- **Wilonet** — mutual respect, arrived at almost instantly. They conferred in Dwarvish about whether the job was worth taking before either of them had said a friendly word to the human. She spent her turn in the Nightroot Asp fight standing shoulder to shoulder with him, missed, put a sword scar across his armor, and then fed him the potion that brought him back from the floor.
- **Icarus** — has said essentially nothing to him. When [Jonas Dawn](../npcs/jonas-dawn.md) asked Grano to vouch for Icarus, Grano answered honestly: *"I don't know him that well."* Icarus has now healed under his hands and killed the thing that put him on the ground, and their entire exchange about it was *"Grano. Thank you, Grano."*
- **[Jonas Dawn](../npcs/jonas-dawn.md)** — introduced himself politely, asked the real question about why Miriam had gone, and was the one Jonas trusted with a healing potion — along with the instruction not to waste it on Icarus.
