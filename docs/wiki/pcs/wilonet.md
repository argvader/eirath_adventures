# Wilonet

**Player:** Lindsey · **Ancestry:** Drow · **Class:** Rogue

*Outlander*

## Overview

Three hundred years old and comfortably the eldest thing in the party, which she does not let anyone forget. Tall, with pale skin tinted blue-violet, long silver hair, a green hood and cloak, and light metallic armor built for moving quietly. She lives on the outskirts of [Timberfall](../locations/timberfall.md) rather than in it, comes into town only when she needs to, and has been ranging the shallow edges of the [Graylight Forest](../locations/graylight-forest.md) long enough to have her own hunter's traps still set out there.

She took the job for money. She wants a new sword. She has been perfectly honest about this from the first minute and has not pretended otherwise since.

## Temperament

Dry, cutting, and unexpectedly decent underneath it. She refers to Icarus, without affection, as "the smelly one" and "the human," and proposed within five minutes of meeting him that if he slowed them down they should leave him behind and split his share. When she found him about to step into one of her own snares, she admits the thought of letting him do it *crossed her mind* — and then she stopped him, warned him what her traps look like, and threw a rock to spring it so he'd know for next time.

She speaks Dwarvish. She used it, in front of the Silverbriers' man, to ask Grano privately whether this job was worth taking.

## In Combat

The most dangerous person in the party by a considerable margin, and the fight in the Graylight made that undeniable. Short sword in hand and a dagger in reserve, a dexterity of 20, and a habit of ending things. She **beheaded** one blinking wolf with a single clean stroke, and when a second one turned on Grano she took it apart deliberately — opening it from the throat down and spilling it while holding eye contact with the surviving wolf.

It worked. The wolf was afraid of her.

The Nightroot Asp went differently. It bit her before initiative was even rolled, opened her
arm, and pumped enough venom into her that she needed a constitution save to keep it out of her
blood. She drank a potion, walked into the melee to stand beside Grano, and then **missed
twice** — a natural 1 that put a sword scar across the dwarf's armor instead of the snake, and
a thrown rock that bounced off the thing's skull and hit Icarus in the eye. She spent the rest
of the fight standing back and letting them have it, which she announced out loud.

Her apology afterward was to Grano, and it was specific: *"I'm sorry it startled me, and I
missed both of my shots. Glad you're okay."*

## The Map

The [Silverbriers](../factions/silverbrier-family.md) handed the party a map drawn by a man who
had, in the head of security's own words, only recently learned the letter X. Wilonet has
started **correcting it as they go** — she is good with maps, and she has been marking in the
things that are actually out there. The wolf attack. The snake. The
[logging camp](../locations/the-logging-camp.md).

It is the only accurate record of the deep Graylight anyone in Timberfall has ever had.

## Relationships

- **Icarus** — she despises him with real energy, and has still saved his life once and bought back the goodwill he destroyed twice. She promised [Jonas Dawn](../npcs/jonas-dawn.md) she would try to rescue his sister *as an apology* for Icarus's behavior. When the forest dragged something out of his past and left him shaking in the fog, she was the one who told him there is a magic here and not to let it take him: *"Focus on the present, or there won't be a future."* He told her he was fine.
- **Grano** — an easy, immediate, wordless respect. They spoke Dwarvish together before they had spoken common. She would rather have his counsel than the human's leadership, and let Icarus "lead" mainly because arguing was beneath her. She used her whole action to pour a potion down his throat while he was rolling death saves, and it cost her a fight she was already losing.
- **[Old Marnie](../npcs/old-marnie.md)** — a genuine old friend, one of the few she has. She asked the others to let her do the talking and asked them, specifically, not to rough him up.
- **[Jonas Dawn](../npcs/jonas-dawn.md)** — she heard his private grief across a crowded tavern on a natural twenty, and her first instinct was to shout across the room that the price for rescuing his sister had just gone up to forty gold. Her second, later, was to promise to bring the woman home.

**[I Think We're Still Here](../../sessions/2026-07-19.md)** — Took first watch both nights on the grounds that she does not sleep, and set the rotation so the wounded got the unbroken rest. Read the bite marks on the blink dog corpses as **humanoid, not canine**. Remembered [Old Marnie](../npcs/old-marnie.md)'s warning, stopped to check the nightroot for a trick — and was bitten anyway by the thing pretending to be it. Had the worst combat of her life and said so. Brought Grano back off the floor with a potion. Chose the trail: of the two sets of armored footprints leaving the camp, she took the smaller one, reasoning it was [Miriam](../npcs/miriam-dawn.md) and that finding her first was the way to find everyone. She is the only one of the three who has had **no dreams at all**.
