# Halvik Dern

**Foreman of the missing logging crew — [the Cut Site](../locations/the-logging-camp.md), [Graylight Forest](../locations/graylight-forest.md)**

## Overview

The man the [Silverbriers](../factions/silverbrier-family.md) put in charge of twelve
loggers and sent two or three days into the [Graylight](../locations/graylight-forest.md).
The party has never met him. They have read him — a worn journal left open on a desk in an
abandoned camp, half a dozen entries long, which is the only account anybody has of what
happened out there.

He was a competent foreman. The journal is orderly, dated, and unsentimental right up until
the point where it stops being any of those things.

## What He Wrote

The entries, in order, are a record of a man watching his crew stop being people.

- **Arrival.** Good time out from [Timberfall](../locations/timberfall.md). Twelve total —
  the standard crew from Timberfall proper, two hacksmen hired from the southern camps, and
  one orcish woman off Graylight's outer trade roads. *Strong arms. Doesn't talk a lot.*
  Forest thicker than the map promised, but not unworkable. They set a perimeter and marked
  the first line for cutting.
- **The fog.** *"Been a strange fog in the morning. Not heavy — just persistent. Like it
  refuses to move out of the way."*
- **The stopping.** Productivity fell off before midday. He blamed fatigue, travel, new air,
  and did not believe himself. *Some of the men just stopped working. It's like they don't
  have the will to work anymore.*
- **The refusals.** A few refused outright. Said **the wood feels wrong.** Said **the trees
  watch us back.** He disciplined them and reminded them of their contract terms, and it
  worked, and he wrote down that morale was unstable anyway.
- **[Cara](cara.md).** The orcish woman stopped mid-swing, put her forehead against a tree,
  said nothing, and then began beating her head into the trunk until she bled. They tried to
  reach her. She pushed them off and walked into the forest. **Two others followed her.**
- **The last entry.** Written by a man alone:

> *I don't understand why I'm here. I'm the only one left. Where have all the others gone?
> I guess I'll go look for them and see where they are. I hope people can find us if we go
> missing. I think we're still alive. I think we're still here.*
>
> — signed, foreman Halvik Dern

## Status

**Missing.** He went into the trees after his own crew and did not come back to the desk he
left the journal on. Whether the last entry was written before or after he stopped being able
to tell is an open question — *I think we're still alive* is not the sentence of a man who is
certain of it.

The night after the party read it, all three of them saw
[figures](../locations/the-logging-camp.md) working that camp who were not there.
