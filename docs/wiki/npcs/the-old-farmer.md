# The Old Farmer

**Dead — a farmhouse on the road to [Timberfall](../locations/timberfall.md)**

## Overview

A kindly old man with a gray beard and a bad hip who opened his door to a stranger on the
road, fed him, housed him, refused his money, and was killed in his own cellar for it.

He does not appear in the party's present. [Icarus](../pcs/icarus.md) relived him in a
flashback dragged up by the [Graylight Veil](../locations/graylight-forest.md), somewhere in
the fog on the fourth day, and said nothing about it to anyone afterward.

## What Happened

Icarus was days late on the road to Timberfall, sleeping rough, and the voice in his head had
been asking for **sustenance** the whole way. The farmhouse was the first roof he'd seen.

The old man was delighted to have him. *"Oh, hi, traveler. You look exhausted — come inside,
come inside."* He gave Icarus a place to wash and change, waved away the gold he was offered,
and said the thing that a person only says when it is true: *"It's been nice having someone to
talk to again."*

At one or two in the morning [the voice](the-voice.md) woke Icarus up and told him to go
downstairs.

He found the old man in the cellar, chopping. And the old man was not bent anymore — the arms
were **big, strong**, nothing like the frame that had been holding its hip at the door — and
he was cutting up what Icarus took for a human corpse. Asked what he thought he was doing, the
farmer turned around, honestly puzzled:

> *What do you mean, what am I doing? I always prepare food for my guest.*

Icarus kicked him down the stairs and drew his sword. The old man crawled and begged — *"Help
me, please. Please. Why are you leaving?"* — and Icarus told him he was leaving because he was
chopping up a person, and the old man said, *"That's not a person. What are you talking
about?"*

The voice said **kill him**, and *slice his throat and sustain me*, and Icarus felt his own
arms thicken and his feet go light. He came down the stairs with the sword. His other hand
went out on its own, and his magic — for the very first time — went off, and the farmer's face
came apart.

## Afterward

The blood ran up the blade and **into** it. The corpse on the block resolved into what it had
always been: **venison. A deer.** And the arms on the body at the foot of the stairs were old
man's arms again, thin, no muscle on them at all.

Then the voice, satisfied:

> *We will keep doing this, Icarus. But you will keep getting stronger. And if you don't, your
> mother's life is forfeit.*

## What He Actually Was

Nothing, most likely. There is no evidence that the farmer was ever anything but an old man
butchering a deer for a guest who was hearing another creature's voice. Every monstrous detail
— the arms, the corpse, the menace on the stairs — corrected itself the moment the killing was
finished, which is a pattern worth noticing: the thing in Icarus **showed him a monster**, and
took a life the second he swung at it.

[Old Marnie](old-marnie.md) told the party, before they ever entered the forest, not to trust
everything they see out there. Nobody has yet told Icarus that the same advice applies to the
inside of his own skull.
