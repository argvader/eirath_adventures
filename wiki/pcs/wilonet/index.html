    Drow Rogue — Outlander¶

    Overview¶

    Three hundred years old and comfortably the eldest thing in the party, which she does not let anyone forget. Tall, with pale skin tinted blue-violet, long silver hair, a green hood and cloak, and light metallic armor built for moving quietly. She lives on the outskirts of Timberfall rather than in it, comes into town only when she needs to, and has been ranging the shallow edges of the Graylight Forest long enough to have her own hunter's traps still set out there.

    She took the job for money. She wants a new sword. She has been perfectly honest about this from the first minute and has not pretended otherwise since.

    Temperament¶

    Dry, cutting, and unexpectedly decent underneath it. She refers to Icarus, without affection, as "the smelly one" and "the human," and proposed within five minutes of meeting him that if he slowed them down they should leave him behind and split his share. When she found him about to step into one of her own snares, she admits the thought of letting him do it crossed her mind — and then she stopped him, warned him what her traps look like, and threw a rock to spring it so he'd know for next time.

    She speaks Dwarvish. She used it, in front of the Silverbriers' man, to ask Grano privately whether this job was worth taking.

    In Combat¶

    The most dangerous person in the party by a considerable margin, and the fight in the Graylight made that undeniable. Short sword in hand and a dagger in reserve, a dexterity of 20, and a habit of ending things. She beheaded one blinking wolf with a single clean stroke, and when a second one turned on Grano she took it apart deliberately — opening it from the throat down and spilling it while holding eye contact with the surviving wolf.

    It worked. The wolf was afraid of her.

    Relationships¶

    • Icarus — she despises him with real energy, and has still saved his life once and bought back the goodwill he destroyed twice. She promised Jonas Dawn she would try to rescue his sister as an apology for Icarus's behavior.
    • Grano — an easy, immediate, wordless respect. They spoke Dwarvish together before they had spoken common. She would rather have his counsel than the human's leadership, and let Icarus "lead" mainly because arguing was beneath her.
    • Old Marnie — a genuine old friend, one of the few she has. She asked the others to let her do the talking and asked them, specifically, not to rough him up.
    • Jonas Dawn — she heard his private grief across a crowded tavern on a natural twenty, and her first instinct was to shout across the room that the price for rescuing his sister had just gone up to forty gold. Her second, later, was to promise to bring the woman home.

    Session History¶

    Twelve Missing — Negotiated, listened, and killed. Recognized the eavesdropping opportunity at the Fat Goat and used it. Got the party its only real information about the forest by taking them to Old Marnie. Kept Icarus out of her own trap. Killed two blinking wolves, one by beheading and one by disembowelment, and frightened a third badly enough that it mattered.
